Weakly vs Strongly Polynomial Algorithms for LP

LP with $$n$$ variables, $$m$$ constraints
$$L$$: encoding length of the input.

weakly polynomial
  • $$\mathrm{poly}(m,n,L)$$ basic arithmetic operations.
  • Standard variants of Ellipsoid and interior point methods: running time bound heavily relies on $$L$$.
strongly polynomial
  • $$\mathrm{poly}(m,n)$$ basic arithmetic operations.
  • PSPACE: all numbers occurring in the algorithm must remain polynomially bounded in input size.

Fast Weakly Polynomial Algorithms for LP

$$\varepsilon$$-approximate solution
  • Approximately optimal: $$\langle c, x \rangle\le\mathrm{OPT}+\varepsilon \|c\| R$$
  • Approximately feasible: $$\|Ax-b\|\le\varepsilon (\|A\|_F R+\|b\|)$$
  • $$\log(1/\varepsilon)$$ dependence $$\Rightarrow$$ exact algorithm with $$L$$ dependence.

Tardos's framework: variable fixing

$$$\min \langle c, x \rangle\, Ax=b,\, x\ge 0, A \in \Z^{m \times n}$$$ Running time dependent only on constraint matrix $$A$$, but not on $$b$$ and $$c$$.
Key idea for the first strongly polynomial algorithm for minimum cost flows.

Proximity Use exact solvers to find optimal solution $$x$$ to $$\varepsilon$$-rounded (perturbed) problems. Proximity yields that an optimal solution $$x^*$$ to the original problem is within $$\mathrm{poly}(n)\Delta \cdot \varepsilon$$ of the rounded problem.
Variable Fixing If the proximity is better than $$\|x\|_\infty$$, then we learn $$x^*_i > 0$$ for a variable and so the corresponding slack variable is $$s^*_i = 0$$.
$$\leadsto$$ delete variable and recurse on smaller problem.

Our contributions: Dadush–Natura–V. '20

Generalizing Tardos' result to real matrices We give a blackbox algorithm that can handle any real matrix $$A\in \R^{m \times n}$$ and dependence $$\log\bar\chi_A$$ instead of $$\log \Delta_A$$.

Usage of approximate solvers We only require any approximate LP solver, and can directly leverage the fast approximate LP algorithms. $$O(mn^{\omega+1+o(1)}\log\bar\chi_A)$$ exact deterministic LP algorithm using van den Brand '20.

Certificates for infeasibility and large condition numbers If primal or dual linear programming are infeasible we provide a Farkas certificate. In case that the condition number is larger than our guess, we are able to provide a certificate.

$$\bar\chi$$ is hard to estimate. Iterative guesses $$M\to \max\{M^2,\textrm{certified lower bound at failure}\}$$.

$$$\min \langle c, x \rangle\, Ax=b,\, x\ge 0, A \in \Z^{m \times n}$$$

Tardos '86
  • Solves LP via $$O(mn)$$ calls to an exact solver for $$\min \langle \tilde c, x \rangle\, Ax=\tilde b,\, x\ge 0, A \in \Z^{m \times n}$$.
  • $$\tilde b,\tilde c$$ integer vectors with entries $$O(n^2\Delta)$$
  • Key property: in a basic solution $$x$$, we have $$x_i=0$$ or $$x_i> 1/(n^{O(1)}\Delta_A)$$ for every $$i\in [n]$$.
  • Inherently relies on integrality arguments

DNV '20
  • $$O(mn)$$ calls to approximate LP
  • $$\log\bar\chi_A$$ dependence with no integrality required.

The mysterious $$\bar\chi_A$$

through a matroidal lens

The condition number $$\bar\chi_A$$

$$$ \bar\chi_A:=\sup\left\{\|A^\top \left(A D A^\top\right)^{-1}AD\|\, : D\in {\mathbf D}\right\} $$$
  • Introduced by Dikin '67, Stewart '89, Todd '90, ...
  • Bounds norm of oblique projections.
  • Depends only on the subspace $$\ker(A)$$.
  • Plays key role in certain interior point methods.

The circuit imbalance measure

...the "combinatorial" sister of $$\bar\chi_A$$

A circuit of $$A$$ is a minimal linearly dependent subset of columns $$C \subseteq [n]$$ . Let $$\mathcal{C}$$ denote the set of all circuits.
The circuit imbalance measure of $$A$$ is $$$\kappa_A:= \max\left\{\left|\frac{g_j}{g_i} \right|: Ag=0,\, \mathrm{supp}(g) \in \mathcal{C}, i,j\in \mathrm{supp}(g),\,\right\}$$$
If $$A$$ is a TU-matrix, then $$\kappa_A=1$$. More generally, if $$A$$ is integer, then $$\kappa_A\le \Delta_A$$.
Proof. For a TU-matrix, $$Ax=0$$, $$-1\le x\le 1$$, $$x_J=0$$ is an integer polytope for all $$J\subseteq n$$. The second part follows by Cramer's rule.
[DHNV20] $$\sqrt{1+\kappa_A^2}\le \bar\chi_A \le n\kappa_A.$$ Thus, $$\log (n+\kappa_A)=\Theta (\log(n+\bar\chi_A))$$.

$$\Delta$$ vs $$\kappa$$

  • In general $$\kappa \le n\Delta$$.
  • For complete undirected graph: $$$\kappa = 2,\text{ but } \Delta \ge 2^{\lfloor n/3 \rfloor}$$$
as $$$\det \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ \end{pmatrix} = 2$$$

Near-optimal rescaling

  • $$A\in \R^{m\times n}$$. Let $$\mathbf{D}$$ denote the set of $$n\times n$$ positive diagonal matrices.
  • Diagonal rescaling (LP') of (LP): Replace $$A'=AD$$, $$c'=Dc$$, $$b'=b$$ for some $$D\in \mathbf{D}$$.
  • Natural invariance of the central path and standard IPMs.
  • Optimized versions of the condition numbers:
    $$\bar\chi_A^* := \inf\{\bar\chi_{AD}: D\in \mathbf{D}\}\ ,\quad \kappa_A^* := \inf\{\kappa_{AD}: D\in \mathbf{D}\}.$$
Finding a nearly-optimal rescaling of $$A$$ DHNV '20 Given $$A \in \R^{m \times n}$$, in $$O(n^2m^2 + n^3)$$ time, we can compute
  1. rescaling $$D \in \mathbf{D}$$ satisfying $$ \bar\chi_A^*\le \bar\chi_{AD}\le n (\bar\chi_A^*)^3$$.
  2. $$t \geq 1$$ satisfying $$t \leq \chi_A \leq n(\bar{\chi}_A^*)^2t$$.
  • In all algorithms we can replace $$\log(n+\bar\chi_A)$$ dependence by $$\log(n+\bar\chi_A^*)$$ dependence.
  • Recall that it is NP-hard to approximate $$\bar\chi_A$$ within a factor $$2^{\mathrm{poly}(\mathrm{rank}(A))}$$: Tunçel '99

Proximity theorems for $$\kappa_A$$

Linear Programming in subspace view

...a change of perspective

In standard form for $$A \in \mathbb{Q}^{m \times n}$$, $$b\in \mathbb{Q}^m$$, $$c\in \mathbb{Q}^n$$, $$$ \begin{aligned} \min \; &\langle c, x \rangle & \max \; & \langle y, b \rangle \\ Ax& =b & \qquad A^\top y + s &= c \\ x &\geq 0 & s & \geq 0 \\ \end{aligned} $$$

In subspace view for $$W = \ker(A)$$, $$d \in \mathbb{Q}^n$$, s.t. $$Ad=b$$, $$$ \begin{aligned} \min \; &\langle c, x\rangle & \max \; & \langle c - s, d \rangle \\ x& \in W + d & \qquad s &\in W^\perp + c \\ x &\geq 0 & s & \geq 0 \\ \end{aligned} $$$

Hoffman proximity theorem

Assume that the system $$x \in W + d, x \ge 0$$ is feasible. Then there exists a feasible solution such that $$\|x - d\|_\infty \le \kappa_W \|d^-\|_1$$.
Proof sketch.
  • Take any feasible $$x \in W + d, x \ge 0$$. Thus,
    $$x-d\in W$$.
  • We decompose $$x - d=g_1+g_2+\ldots+g_t$$ into sign-consistent circuits $$g_i\in W$$ by Carathéodory's theorem.
  • Delete circuits that do not intersect $$\textrm{supp}(d^-)$$.
  • For all other circuits $$g$$ and indices $$j$$,
    $$|g_{j}|\le \kappa_A|g_{k}|$$ for some $$k\in \textrm{supp}(d^-)$$.

Variable fixing for feasibility

Assume that the system $$x \in W + d, x \ge 0$$ is feasible. Then there exists a feasible solution such that $$\|x - d\|_\infty \le \kappa_W \|d^-\|_1$$.
Recursive algorithm
  • Use approximate solver to get near feasible
    $$z\in W+d$$ with $$\|z^-\|_1$$ "small".
  • $$I:=\{i\in [n]:\, z_i> \kappa_W \|z^-\|_1\}$$.
  • $$J:=\{i\in [n]:\, z_i\le \kappa_W \|z^-\|_1\}$$.
  • By proximity, there exists a feasible solution with $$x_I>0$$.
  • Recurse on the subspace $$W'=\mathrm{proj}_J(W)$$ with $$d'=d_J$$.
  • If $$W=\ker(A)$$, then we obtain $$W'=\ker(A')$$ by eliminating the variables in $$I$$.
$$$ \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 & | & & \\ 0 & 1 & | & A' & \\ 0 & 0 & |& & \\ 0 & 0 & |& & \\ \end{pmatrix} $$$

Variable fixing for feasibility

Recursive algorithm
  • Use approximate solver to get near feasible
    $$z\in W+d$$ with $$\|z^-\|_1$$ "small".
  • $$I:=\{i\in [n]:\, z_i> \kappa_W \|z^-\|_1\}$$.
  • $$J:=\{i\in [n]:\, z_i\le \kappa_W \|z^-\|_1\}$$.
  • Recurse on the subspace $$W'=\mathrm{proj}_J(W)$$ with $$d'=d_J$$.
Questions
  • How do we guarantee that $$I\neq \emptyset$$?
  • How can we construct a feasible solution? Given $$x'\in \mathrm{proj}_J(W)+d_J$$, $$x'\ge 0$$, how do we recover $$x\in W+d$$, $$x\ge 0$$?

The lifting operation

  • $$W\subseteq \mathbb{R}^n$$ subspace, $$J\subseteq [n]$$
  • $$y\in \mathrm{proj}_J(W)$$, i.e. $$\exists x\in W$$, $$x_J=y$$.
  • The lifting of $$y$$ to $$W$$ is defined as $$$L_J^W(y):=\argmin_x\left\{\|x\|_2:\, x\in W, x_J=y\right\}$$$
  • Can be computed using a projection matrix.
$$\|L_J^W(y)\|_\infty\le \kappa_W \|y\|_1$$.
Proof. A similar circuit decomposition argument.

The feasibility algorithm

Oracle$$(\varepsilon)$$ $$ \begin{aligned} z &\in W + d\\ \|z^-\|_\infty &\le \varepsilon \|d^-\|_1\\ {\color{blue} \|z - d\|_\infty} &{\color{blue}\le C\kappa_W \|d^-\|_1} \end{aligned} $$

Feasibility$$(W,d)$$ $$$ \begin{aligned} x &\in W + d,\quad x\ge 0 \\ { \color{blue} \|x - d\|_\infty }& { \color{blue}\le C'\kappa_W^2 n \|d^-\|_1} \end{aligned} $$$ Stronger system with proximity constraint useful for "pullback"
  • Obtain $$z$$ by applying the oracle with
    $$\varepsilon = 1/(\kappa \cdot \mathrm{poly}(n))$$
  • $$J := \{i\in [n] :\, z_i < \kappa_W \|z^-\|_1\}$$.
  • If $$J=\emptyset$$ then replace $$d$$ by the projection $$d/W$$.
  • Apply the recursive solver to $$\mathrm{proj}_J(W)$$ and $$z_J$$ to obtain
    $$\tilde x \in \mathrm{proj}_J(W) + z_J, \ \tilde x \ge 0$$ .
  • Lift the solution back up to obtain $$$ x := z + L_J^W(\tilde x - z_J) \ge 0.$$$
  • Non-negativity and proximity follows from proximity of the recursive solver!

The feasibility algorithm

  • As described above, we need $$\le n$$ calls to the oracle.
  • Can be decreased to $$\le m$$ calls (with a little more care.)
  • This leads to an $$O(mn^{\omega+o(1)}\log(\kappa_W+n))$$ feasibility algorithm using van den Brand '20.
Estimating and certifying $$\kappa_W$$
  • We maintain a guess $$M$$ on $$\kappa_W$$.
  • If $$\|L_J^W(y)\|_\infty\le M\|y\|_1$$ for every lifting call, the algorithm succeeds.
  • Otherwise, we can recover a circuit with imbalance $$>M$$, showing that $$\kappa_W>M$$.

Proximal optimal solutions

proximity works for optimization as well!
$$$ \begin{aligned} \min \; &\langle c, x\rangle & \max \; & \langle c - s, d \rangle \\ x& \in W + d & \qquad s &\in W^\perp + c \\ x &\geq 0 & s & \geq 0 \\ \end{aligned} $$$

Let $$s \ge 0, s \in W^\perp + c$$ be a feasible dual, but not necessarily optimal solution.
Assuming that the primal is feasible, there exists an optimal solution $$x \in W + d, x \ge 0$$ such that $$\|x - d\|_\infty \le \kappa_W(\|d^-\|_1 + \|d_{\mathrm{supp}(s)}\|_1)$$.

Optimization algorithm

$$$ \begin{aligned} \min \; &\langle c, x\rangle & \max \; & \langle c - s, d \rangle \\ x& \in W + d & \qquad s &\in W^\perp + c \\ x &\geq 0 & s & \geq 0 \\ \end{aligned} $$$

  • Altogether $$nm$$ calls to the black box solver.
  • We have $$\le n$$ Outer Loops, each comprising $$\le m$$ Inner Loops
  • Each Outer Loop finds $$\tilde d$$ with $$\|d-\tilde d\|$$ "small", and $$(x,s)$$ primal and dual optimal solutions to $$$\min \; \langle c, x\rangle \quad x\in W+\tilde d\, \quad x\ge 0.$$$
  • Using proximity, we can use this to conclude $$x_I>0$$ for a certain variable set $$I\subseteq [n]$$ and recurse.

Constructive Hoffman proximity

More general form of Hoffman proximity theorem
Let $$W \subset \R^n$$ be a subspace and $$\ell,u \in \R^n$$ lower and upper bounds and assume that $$P = \{x \in W: \ell \le x \le u\}$$ is non-empty. Then there exists $$x\in P$$ such that $$$\|x\|_\infty \le \kappa_W(\|\ell^+\|_1 + \|u^-\|_1).$$$
Certifying sometimes requires the following constructive version:
Given some $$y \in P$$ such an $$x \in P$$ with $$\|x\|_\infty \le \kappa_W(\|\ell^+\|_1 + \|u^-\|_1)$$ can be found in $$O(n^3)$$ .
Proof idea. Sign-consistently reduce the norm of $$y$$ while maintaining containment in $$P$$ .

Open questions

  • Feasibility needs $$m$$ calls—can we make it $$\min\{m,n-m\}$$ to have the same for primal and dual?
  • Optimization takes $$mn$$ calls—would fewer be enough?
  • Can we get better for special cases, such as max flow or min-cost flow?
  • Can we get faster (possibly non-deterministic) version of the constructive Hoffman algorithm?
  • Can we extend the black box approach to problems with unbounded $$\kappa$$, such as generalized flows?
